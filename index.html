Land of The Midnight Sun

It is believed that the name of this distinctive country and one of the most breathtaking places on our planet was given by the Old-Norse word Norðrvegr, literally meaning "way to the north.

Norway is famous for green forests and mountains, pristine glaciers, noisy and cool waterfalls, lakes, beautiful fjords, white nights, and the polar lights. The beauty of Norway cannot be described in words!

Norway is also known for its striking landscapes, hunting, fishing, interesting and entertaining history, and culture. All this attracts travel lovers who come to Norway regardless of the season from all over the world.

Where do we go?

10 days of travel and 5 unique places in Norway are waiting for us. Read more about each place from our itinerary below.

Trondheim Bodø Lofoten Islands Tromsø Oslo

Oslo - First point

The Capital and Largest City of Norway

1 night Scandic Hotel

We start our journey from Oslo, the capital of Norway. On a walking tour, you will see the opera house, parliament, the royal palace, and more.

- Dinner included

- Leave your backpack in the hotel

- Transfer from the airport

Trondheim - Second point

The First Capital With the Royal Residence

2 nights Radisson Hotel

The main Christian landmark of Norway, the Nidaros Cathedral is located here. The architectural sign of the new era is a high TV tower with a revolving restaurant.

- Arriving at 9 a.m.

- Instagram-friendly places

- 3-hours walking tour

Bodø - Third point

Unique Fishing Spot in Fascinating Scenery

2 nights Gronheim Guesthouse

We've left "urban" Norway, and Bodø is our first stop for no sightseeing. We will simply go fishing, enjoy the fresh air, and try dishes with salmon and trout.

- Fishing spots

- Fish dinner

- Cable car ride

Lofoten Islands - Fourth point

Stunning Place Beyond the Arctic circle

2 nights Cabinn Hotel

The islands are located in the harsh waters of the Norwegian Sea. It is a land of wild nature, majestic mountains, fjords, and long sandy beaches.

- beaches

- Tasting of local liqueurs

- Boat trip

Tromsø - Final point

Spot for Hunting the Northern Lights

3 nights Scandic Hotel

The largest Norwegian city in the Arctic Circle is also called “Paris of the North”. The main attractions are the Polar Museum and the Arctic Cathedral.

- Free souvenirs

FAQ

Explore our expertly crafted tours

We tried to make our Norway tour as comfortable and interesting as possible. If you still have questions, you can contact us directly or fill out the form below.

Does this tour require physical fitness?

This tour is suitable for untrained tourists. No cliff climbing, we promise.

Will I be able to see the Northern Lights?

We cannot promise you this. The Northern Lights only appear in winter under special circumstances. But we will hunt it in Tromsø.

Can I take my children with me on the tour?

Yes, we offer a 50% discount for children.

Can I join you not in Oslo, but in another city?

Depending on the weather conditions, our route may change, so we strongly recommend starting with other tourists from Oslo. We do not change the route to pick up tourists on the way.
